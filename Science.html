NavIC (Navigation with Indian Constellation)



The Indian Regional Navigation Satellite System (IRNSS) aka NavIC (Navigation with Indian Constellation) is a group of 7 satellites developed by ISRO. This system provides a Global Positioning System (GPS) service in India. It provides real-time positioning and timing services. The system covers areas from India extending up to 1500 kms and is expected to reach more areas in the future.

* NavIC is a satellite navigation system that has contributed to India's satellite technology in several ways, including:

1.Independence

NavIC is an independent, indigenous system that's owned and operated by India. It was developed by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) to reduce India's reliance on foreign satellite systems.

2. Accuracy

NavIC provides positioning accuracy of better than 10 meters in India and better than 20 meters in the surrounding region. This is more accurate than GPS, which has a positional accuracy of 15-20 meters.

3. Applications

NavIC has many applications, including in transportation, agriculture, disaster management, and defense.

4. Services

NavIC offers two services: a Standard Positioning Service (SPS) for civilian users and a Restricted Service (RS) for authorized users.

5. Coverage

NavIC covers India and the surrounding region up to 1,500 kilometers beyond the Indian boundary.

6. Second generation

The second generation of NavIC satellites, including the NVS-01, have improved accuracy, signal resilience, and performance in challenging environments. The NVS-01 also includes an indigenously developed atomic clock to replace the previous Israeli rubidium clock.

7. Chipsets

NavIC is now supported by "Made in India" chipsets.

1. Weather forecasting

NavIC provides atmospheric data that helps improve weather predictions. This helps people prepare for weather events and leads to better preparedness.

3. Monitoring and tracking:

NavIC can track the movement and intensity of natural disasters like cyclones, floods, and earthquakes. Issuing warnings: NavIC data helps authorities issue timely warnings to communities in the path of natural disasters.

4. Coordinating relief efforts:

NavIC helps coordinate relief efforts by ensuring that assistance reaches the right places promptly.

5. Navigation

NavIC provides precise positioning information for navigation in transportation, agriculture, and maritime navigation.

6. Surveying

NavIC helps with land surveying, mapping, and geodetic data collection.

7. Timing

NavIC provides highly accurate timing signals that are vital for sectors like telecommunications and power grid synchronization.
